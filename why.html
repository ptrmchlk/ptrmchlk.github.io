Nostalgia.

Well, partially. The internet I grew up with was actually a noisy mess, overpopulated with dancing GIFs, not text-filled pages.

More reasonable reasons for the minimal websites are:

One – I like it. Maybe the most obvious reason. I find it silent and calming.

Two – I know, more or less, what is here. I'm know very little HTML, but what is here I generally understand. Sure, for a full-blown professional website, there are tools like Hugo and Jekyll. But for a small one like this, I enjoy working on it more when writing directly in HTML.

Three – Maybe because it is this direct interaction with HTML and CSS, but it feels more unmediated and human to me. I feel like I can use it to more directly talk to you, who visits the page. An in the age of the rampant auto-completion, I think its nice.
