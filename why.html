Nostalgia.

Well, partially. The internet I grew up with was a bit of a noisy mess, overpopulated with dancing gifs.

The actual reasons for the minimal websites are:

One - I like the aesthetics of it. The most obvious reason. I find it calming and silent.

Two - I know, more or less, what is here. I'm know very little HTML, but what is here I generally understand. The initial website was using the nice Jekyll generated templates. But I enjoy working on it more in pure HTML.

Three - Maybe because it is this direct interaction with HTML, it feels more unmediated and human, even if simple. An in the age of the rampant auto-completion, I find it just nicer.

Four - Sometimes, for no good reason, I use text based browsers like Lynx. Or simplistic like Dillo. A website like this is clear and accessible.
