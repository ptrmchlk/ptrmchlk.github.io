Hi! Nice to (indirectly) meet you!

I'm Piotr, and I am an evolutionary biologist.

To start at the end, I am interested in communication and its evolutionary implications. How do organisms know what they know, how do they exchange and use information. This can be between two squirrels running around a tree, birches and pines growing near one another, or a fox trying to eat a chicken. More often than not, I am focusing on the interactions between the two sexes. This field is rife with wonderful examples of trying to convey a message through myriad of strategies of every sound, colour and smell — but also with conflict, manipulation and disinformation.

In the past few years I focused on the evolution of seminal fluid proteins, proteins males provide during mating to females in many animal species. These proteins, often thought to be traits used to manipulate females, might be actually beneficial when females use them to adjust their reproduction. Or, at least, so my model suggests. They are also a specific example of post-copulatory sexual selection, a topic I am thinking about more and more, and how it differs from other types of selection. And why.

Broad overview of research interests
